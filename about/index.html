Our Mission

The Japanese art market — spanning paintings, prints, ceramics, lacquerware, swords, and contemporary works — generates over ¥1,000 billion in annual transactions. Yet comprehensive, data-driven market intelligence remains fragmented across individual auction houses, printed catalogues, and private databases.

EDITION exists to unify this fragmented landscape into a single, transparent intelligence platform. We collect, verify, and analyze auction data across Japan's major houses to provide collectors, dealers, researchers, and institutions with the same caliber of market intelligence that has long been available in Western art markets through services like Artnet and Artprice — but tailored specifically to the unique dynamics of the Japanese art ecosystem.

Hedonic Pricing Methodology

EDITION's price estimation is powered by KANTEISHI, a hedonic pricing engine that models artwork value as a function of observable characteristics. Unlike simple comparable-sales approaches, hedonic pricing decomposes price into the contributions of individual attributes — artist reputation, medium, size, auction house, and market conditions — enabling more nuanced and accurate valuations.

Model Architecture

The current model (v0.9) employs an ensemble of HistGradientBoosting and RandomForest regressors trained on 49,968 verified auction results. The ensemble approach reduces variance and improves robustness across different market segments.

1
Feature Engineering (17 Variables)
Each lot is encoded with 17 features: artist global average, median, and CV (coefficient of variation); lot count; medium classification (18 categories); physical dimensions; area; auction house indicator; estimate; estimate-to-average ratio; artist price rank; and market position indicators.
2
Sample Weighting
Low-price lots (under ¥100,000) receive 2x weight; lots under ¥500,000 receive 1.5x weight. This corrects systematic over-estimation in the lower price range — a known challenge when training on log-transformed prices dominated by high-value outliers.
3
Ensemble Training
HistGradientBoosting (max_iter=2000, learning_rate=0.015, max_depth=8) and RandomForest (500 trees) are trained independently. Final predictions average both models' outputs for reduced variance. 5-fold cross-validation confirms generalization: CV R² = 0.864 ± 0.004.
4
Confidence Calibration
Each estimate includes a confidence range based on the artist's historical price variance and the number of comparable lots. Estimates for well-represented artists with 50+ lots carry tighter confidence bands than those for emerging or rarely-traded artists.

Known Limitations

Shinwa Accuracy Gap
Shinwa's public data lacks estimate, medium, and size fields — the three most predictive features. The model relies on proxy estimates derived from the same artist's results at other houses, which inherently limits accuracy. Detailed Shinwa data is available via their member-only SEARCH WEB service.
Low-Price Bias
Despite v0.9's sample weighting improvement, lots under ¥50,000 still exhibit +65% over-estimation on average. This structural bias stems from limited distinguishing features in the lowest price tier and the log-transform's compression of absolute differences.
Estimate Dependency
The auction house estimate (log_estimate) contributes 86.3% of the model's predictive power. When estimates are unavailable, accuracy drops significantly. This is expected — auction house specialists effectively embed expert judgment that cannot be replicated from observable features alone.

Our Principles

Accuracy Over Volume
We will never sacrifice data accuracy for record count. Every data point must be traceable to a verifiable source. Information without evidence is excluded.
Transparent Limitations
We publish our model's known weaknesses alongside its strengths. A pricing model that conceals its failure modes is more dangerous than no model at all.
Continuous Improvement
The model is versioned and continuously retrained as new auction data becomes available. Each version's improvements and regressions are documented and trackable.
Real Data Only
EDITION publishes only verified auction results from licensed Japanese auction houses. No synthetic data, no AI-generated content, no unverified claims.
